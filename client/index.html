About me

Born in the age of the booming internet, my first computer was a Windows 95 that I inherited at the ripe age of 7 years old. By the time I was 10 years old, I had done my first series of Python tutorials, ran my own successful website on how to play Pokemon, and learned how to make viruses and destroyed my own computer.

Things are a little different now. Inspired by my early successes with creating and maintaining a website, and chagrined with the destruction of my own computer, I vowed to forever use my powers to benefit people and the world.

After graduating with my B.S. in Psychology, and pursued my dream of living in Japan for half a decade and teaching in public education, I continued sharpening my skill set, studying computer science and algorithms. I contributed to and implemented features in several open source projects. I built full stack web applications as well as mobile applications.

As I continue growing my skills and learning to speak the language of robots, my dream is to connect with people and projects that allow me to use my skills to do good in the world, such as work in non profits promoting health, alternative energy, or ending world poverty.

When Iâ€™m not engrossed in building software, you can find me brushing up my Japanese, bombing the sidewalk a little too fast on my longboard, or searching for the perfect sushi roll.
